About the project

Dhanbad, known as the Coal Capital of India, is one of the largest industrial towns of Jharkhand State. It has coal mines occupying nearly half of the area under the control of Dhanbad Municipal Corporation. In this context, a proposed loan from the World Bank would be intended partly to improve the city’s road network, and public transport systems. The Comprehensive Mobility Plan for Dhanbad, a master plan prepared by the government of Jharkhand, and consultations with multiple stakeholders led to the formulation of this urban infrastructure development project. The World Bank South Asian Regional Unit engaged the NYU Capstone team to review the Comprehensive Mobility Plan and the associated project report containing the consultant’s assessments on the scope and magnitude of the proposed road improvements. In January 2017, the Capstone team traveled to Dhanbad, where they conducted multiple site visits and interviews with key stakeholders. Data gathered from this fieldwork was used to identify areas for improvement and advance project goals. Ultimately, the Capstone team's research supported the development of innovative initiatives —such as contextual planning, multi-purpose programming, solar lighting, and other urban design solutions—that will help tackle the social, and environmental challenges of implementing the Dhanbad Roads project.

The study area

This preliminary report compiles observations gathered by the team during a series of site visits surrounding. Phase One of the Dhanbad roads project. All initial analysis and remarks will focus on a 400-meter segment of Road 13 located between Birsa Munda Chowk and Joraphatak Chowk (J13-4). Road 13 displays a diverse array of land-uses and users along the streets as they are shared by pedestrians, cars, rickshaws, auto-rickshaws, trucks and street vendors. The distinctive nature of Road 13 was highlighted by the Dhanbad roads’ DPR land use map which is exemplified by the selected 400-meter segment.

Data

Given the fact that Dhanbad Roads project is an urban infrastructure development initiative, it is crucial to analyze both the impacts of roadway upgrades on the existing line use and the potential alteration of traffic patterns. Our team estimates the creation of a commercial corridor along Bank road (and specifically along the segment between Joraphatak Chowk and Birsa Munda Chowk junctions) and flags a potential increase in accidents involving pedestrians and two-wheel vehicles. These projections, however, will be refined by including the data gathered by the team in the city of Dhanbad.
